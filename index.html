Frequently Asked Questions

How long before travel should I get the rabies antibody test?

This depends on your destination country. Most countries require the test to be performed at least 3-6 months before travel. Countries like Australia and Japan require a 180-day waiting period after a successful test, while the EU requires 90 days. We recommend planning well in advance and consulting with us about your specific destination's requirements.

What is the minimum age for pets to have this test?

Pets must usually be at least 12 weeks old for rabies vaccination, and the blood sample is typically taken at least 30 days after vaccination. Therefore, the minimum age is generally around 16 weeks (4 months). Age requirements may vary by country, so we'll provide guidance specific to your destination.

What happens if my pet fails the test?

If your pet's antibody level is below 0.5 IU/ml (the internationally accepted threshold), they will need a booster vaccination and another blood test after 30 days. Our team will provide guidance throughout this process to ensure success on the subsequent test. This is why planning ahead is crucial, allowing time for potential retesting if necessary.

Do all countries require this test?

No, not all countries require a rabies antibody titre test. Countries like the USA and Canada typically require only a valid rabies vaccination certificate. However, most rabies-free or rabies-controlled countries like Australia, Japan, Singapore, Taiwan, and the European Union (for pets coming from high-risk countries) do require this test. We can advise on the specific requirements for your destination.

How long does it take to get results?

Laboratory processing typically takes 2-3 weeks, though this can vary based on current demand and sample shipping time. We recommend planning well in advance of your travel date to accommodate for potential delays or the need for retesting. As soon as we receive the results, we'll contact you immediately and prepare the necessary documentation.
